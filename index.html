üìã PRE-LECTURE SETUP

5 Minutes Before Session

Technical Setup:

  • Open presentation in fullscreen (F11)
  • Test keyboard/clicker - Spacebar advances slides
  • Check projector focus and screen position
  • Ensure internet connection (for emergencies)
  • Have backup: Save presentation as PDF

Physical Setup:

  • Water bottle within reach (but off to side)
  • Phone on silent/airplane mode
  • Handouts distributed or ready to distribute
  • Board markers tested (if needed)
  • Good standing position identified (center, visible to all)

Mental Preparation:

  • Deep breaths - 3 slow inhales/exhales
  • Positive self-talk: "I'm here to help, not impress"
  • Visualize your opening line
  • Remember: Students WANT you to succeed
  • Embrace the nerves - it means you care!
üéØ Pro Tip: Arrive 15 minutes early. Walk the room. Find "friendly faces" in the audience - 3-4 engaged-looking students you can make eye contact with during the session.

Your Opening Stance

  • Position: Center stage, facing audience
  • Posture: Shoulders back, feet shoulder-width apart
  • Hands: Relaxed at sides or holding clicker/notes
  • Expression: Smile - even if nervous!
  • Eye Contact: Scan room, don't fixate on one spot
üéØ If You're Really Nervous: For the first 30 seconds, focus on the back wall instead of faces. Then gradually shift to looking at students. Your brain will calm down once you start speaking.

üéÆ GAME FORMAT

This is your first interactive game. High energy required!

What's on Screen:

üéÆ GAME 1: Front or Back? TEAM FRONT TEAM BACK Left Side Right Side of Room of Room I'll describe something in an app... Your team SHOUTS if it's FRONT or BACK!

What You SAY - Game Setup:

"TEAM FRONT shouts 'FRONT!' and TEAM BACK shouts 'BACK!' Fastest correct team gets a point. Make sense?"

[Wait for nods]

"Alright, let's start easy. Ready?"

[Pause, build anticipation - 2 seconds]

The Game Rounds (Call these out with HIGH energy):

ROUND 1:

"The colorful buttons you click on the screen!"

[Wait for shouts - should be FRONT]

"FRONT! Correct! Team Front gets a point! That's what you SEE - that's frontend!"

ROUND 2:

"Calculating your loan interest rate!"

[Wait for shouts - should be BACK]

"BACK! Yes! That's processing, that's logic - backend! Team Back, one point!"

ROUND 3:

"The login page design with logo and colors!"

[Wait - should be FRONT]

"FRONT! Absolutely! Visual design = frontend!"

ROUND 4:

"Checking if your password is correct when you type it!"

[Wait - should be BACK]

"BACK! Good job! That's verification, that's security logic - backend!"

ROUND 5:

"The animation when you click 'Send Money' - the spinning circle!"

[Wait - should be FRONT]

"FRONT! That visual feedback = frontend!"

ROUND 6:

"Actually transferring ‚Çπ500 from your account to your friend!"

[Wait - should be BACK]

"BACK! Perfect! The ACTUAL transaction, the money movement - that's backend!"

Game Wrap-Up:

"Excellent! Let's tally - Team Front got 3 points, Team Back got 3 points. It's a tie! But you ALL win because you just learned something crucial."

[Pause for effect - smile]

"You now understand the difference between FRONTEND and BACKEND. Let me show you the full picture."

üîß TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

If Students Are Not Engaged:

  • Call on someone directly: "Hey, you in the blue shirt - what do you think?"
  • Physical movement: "Everyone stand up!" (Simon Says game)
  • Poll the room: "Raise hands if..." questions
  • Acknowledge the vibe: "I can see you're tired. Let's do a quick energy break!"

If You're Running Behind (Late):

Skip these slides in order:

  1. Game 4 - Simon Says (Slide 18) - 3 min saved
  2. Game 5 - Future Self (Slide 24) - 3 min saved
  3. Shorten Your Journey to 3 minutes - 4 min saved
  4. Combine Career Paths (Slides 21-22) into one - 2 min saved

Total saved: 12 minutes

If You're Running Ahead (Early):

  • Expand Q&A section at the end
  • Tell more personal stories from your projects
  • Do an extra activity: "Think-Pair-Share" on career goals
  • Live code a tiny example if you're comfortable

If Technology Fails (No Projector):

Backup Plan:

  • Use handouts - have students follow along
  • Draw diagrams on board as you talk
  • Focus more on stories and less on visuals
  • Games still work perfectly without slides!
  • Your authenticity > perfect presentation

If Someone Asks a Question You Don't Know:

"That's a great question! I don't have the exact answer right now, but here's what I do know... [share related knowledge]"

"Connect with me on LinkedIn after this - I'll find the answer and send it to you personally."

Never fake knowledge. Honesty builds trust.

If You Blank Out / Forget What to Say:

  • Look at the slide - it has your cues
  • Pause and sip water - buys you 3 seconds to think
  • Be honest: "Let me think about the best way to explain this..."
  • Ask the audience: "Before I continue, what are YOUR thoughts on this?"
  • Move to next slide if really stuck - keep momentum
